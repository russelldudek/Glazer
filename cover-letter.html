Southern Glazer's / Director of Product Operations, AI/ML
Russell Dudek
Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania

Hiring Team,

Southern Glazer's does not need MLOps as a technical sidecar. The company is operating a large physical distribution network, a meaningful digital commerce platform, and - according to recent public reporting - a commercial model that is shifting toward a combination of field sales, inside sales, and digital commerce while leadership explicitly points to AI as part of adapting to the market. In that environment, the real product is not a pipeline. It is confidence: confidence that a model is ready to release, observable in production, recoverable when conditions change, and connected to a business outcome.

That is the operating problem that interests me. My career has centered on building mechanisms around complex technical work. At Compunetics, I led across engineering, manufacturing, quality, delivery, and high-reliability technical programs, building quality systems, root-cause practices, performance measures, and controls. At Amazon, I built operating visibility, escalation paths, standard work, and leadership routines in a 24/7 customer-backward environment supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually. Today at Vape-Jet, I connect factory, customer, support, engineering, and machine-fleet signals into prioritized work, earlier risk detection, better handoffs, dashboards, training, and accountability in a software-enabled robotics and machine-vision environment with approximately 1,000 fielded robots.

I also bring an AI operating-model lens. Through DudeWorth, I develop AI-readiness and workflow frameworks that connect value, feasibility, data readiness, evaluation, human judgment, escalation, decision records, governance, adoption burden, reuse, and time-to-value. My point of view is that governance only works when it is built into how work moves.

I should be direct about the strongest objection. My background is not a conventional seven-year progression through ML platform operations, and I will not rewrite it into one. The case for me is transferability: director-level leadership in technical and operating systems; deep experience with reliability, quality, controls, escalation, and adoption; current AI-first transformation work; and the ability to build the operating system around a technically strong platform team. If the role requires the director to be the deepest hands-on MLOps engineer in the room, I am not the obvious profile. If it requires a leader who can make reliability, ownership, governance, cross-functional decision rights, and business outcomes cohere around deep technical talent, that is a serious conversation.

My candidate thesis is built around a Model Reliability Contract: every production model should have explicit release evidence, live operating boundaries, named intervention rights, and a business-outcome owner. I have translated that thesis into a 120-day entry plan, decision architecture, hard-objection analysis, and a practical reliability-contract artifact.

I would value the chance to test that thinking against the realities of Southern Glazer's AIML platform organization.

Carpe diem,
Russell Dudek
