Review 01 / Days 1-30

Read the production system.

Objective: understand the actual model lifecycle, ownership seams, platform toil, and consequence landscape.

  • Inventory production models, pipelines, critical decisions, owners, data dependencies, release paths, incidents, vendors, and platform debt.
  • Shadow data science, data engineering, platform operations, IT/security, product, and business workflow owners.
  • Map where release evidence, monitoring, intervention authority, and business-outcome ownership are explicit or missing.
  • Baseline deployment lead time, monitoring coverage, alert quality, detection/recovery, cost visibility, and adoption evidence.
Exit evidence: model/service map - ownership map - baseline scorecard - trust-debt register.
Review 02 / Days 31-60

Define the contract.

Objective: agree on the smallest useful reliability standard and the conditions that justify exceptions.

  • Co-design minimum release evidence by model consequence and use-case class.
  • Define a practical service envelope covering quality, drift, data health, latency, availability, freshness, and cost.
  • Name intervention rights for tune, retrain, rollback, fallback, pause, and escalation.
  • Select one production path where reliability friction and business consequence are visible.
Exit evidence: Contract v0.1 - intervention matrix - prioritized reliability debt - pilot charter.

Review 03 / Days 61-90

Prove one path end to end.

Objective: show that the contract improves a real production path without documentation theater.

  • Apply the contract with accountable technical and business owners.
  • Automate appropriate release checks, monitoring, alert routing, decision evidence, and rollback/fallback preparation.
  • Exercise at least one controlled failure or recovery path.
  • Measure alert quality, time to detect, time to decide, time to recover, deployment flow, cost, adoption, and business-outcome linkage.
  • Capture what should become a platform primitive, a documented exception, or a stopped practice.
Exit evidence: validated path - exercised recovery - measured friction removed - reusable learning record.
Review 04 / Days 91-120

Scale the pattern, not the bureaucracy.

Objective: turn proven controls into reusable capability, a credible roadmap, and a shared operating review.

  • Convert proven checks and runbooks into platform templates with explicit exception logic.
  • Establish a monthly reliability review tied to model health, workflow outcomes, economics, and trust debt.
  • Prioritize platform debt by business consequence and recurring toil rather than technical elegance alone.
  • Build the capability, hiring, training, vendor, and tooling roadmap from observed constraints.
  • Present the next two quarters as choices: standardize, invest, partner, defer, or retire.
Exit evidence: reusable standard - operating review - platform roadmap - talent and capability plan.
